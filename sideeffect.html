Side Effect Desflurane

How to dose Desflurane for children? Suprane (desflurane, USP) is not recommended for induction of anesthesia in pediatric patients because of the high to moderate upper-airway incidence. After induction of anesthesia with agents other than suprane, and tracheal intubation, suprane is indicated as anesthesia maintenance in infants and children. In which dosage is desflurane available? Suprane (desflurane, USP), NDC 10019-641-24, packaged in amber colored bottles containing 240 mL desflurane.

What side effects can be experienced due to Desflurane?

Along with the necessary effects, the drug may cause some undesirable effects. Although not all of these side effects can occur, if they occur they may need medical attention. Some side effects may occur that usually do not require medical attention. These side effects may be lost during treatment as your body adjusts to the medication. Also, your doctor may be able to tell you about ways to prevent or reduce some of the side effects.

A Common Occurrence

  • Cough
  • Nausea or Vomiting
    • -Rarely Happening
  • Dizzy
  • Headache
  • Irritation or red eyes
  • Nervous and restless
  • Sore throat
Not everyone has the following side effects. There may be some side effects not mentioned above. If you have concerns about certain side effects, consult your doctor or pharmacist.

What to know before using Desflurane?

  • Allergy
    • Let your doctor know if you have an unusual or allergic reaction to this drug or other medication. Tell your doctor as well if you have other types of allergies such as food, dyes, preservatives. For non-prescription products, read the label on the packaging carefully.
  • Children
    • Desflurane has been tested in children. Not used to initiate anesthesia in children who are awake for causing irritation and other undesirable effects. However, when used to continue general anesthesia that has started with other anesthesia, desflurane does not cause different side effects or problems in children than it does in adults.
  • Elderly
    • Desflurane has been tested and has not been proven to cause different side effects or problems in the elderly from young adults. However, the elderly usually require a small amount of dope from a younger person.

    Is Desflurane safe for pregnant and lactating women? There is no adequate research on the risks of using this drug in pregnant or lactating women. Always consult your doctor to consider potential benefits and risks before using this medication. These drugs fall into the risk of pregnancy category B according to the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA). The following is the FDA risk category of pregnancy references: • A = No risk, B = Not at risk in some studies, • C = Possibly risky, • D = There is positive evidence of risk, • X = Contraindications, • N = Unknown There is no adequate information about the safety of using this drug during breastfeeding. Always consult your doctor to consider potential benefits and risks before using this drug during breastfeeding.

    What should I do if I miss a dose?

    If you forget a dose of this medicine, drink as soon as possible. But when it comes to the next dose, skip the missed dose and return to the usual dose schedule. Do not double dose.
